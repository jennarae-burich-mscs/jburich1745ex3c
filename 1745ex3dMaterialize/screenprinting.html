Screenprinting

It's our lifeblood and passion, and where Gold Tree began.

The process

It begins with a screen: a fine wire mesh stretched onto a metal frame. The first step is to coat it with emulsion, which covers all of the holes in the screen. Emulsion is light sensitive, and has to dry in a dark room, and can only be exposed to yellow light. After it's dry, your chosen design is burned onto the screen with light. The areas that weren't exposed to light, where your design is, are washed away and like magic, your design appears! The next step is to align it on the press, to make sure the design is going to be printed where it's supposed to be. The last step is where the real magic happens: printing.For each color, ink is applied to the screen, and then pushed across with a squeegee like tool. It is placed under heat so the ink cures, and then ready to wear!If you have a one color design, your whole design will fit on one screen. If you have two or more, each color is made into a seperation, and is on it's own screen. Each screen has to be aligned perfectly with each other, or else it won't turn out at all. This is the reason the more colors you have, the more your design will cost.
